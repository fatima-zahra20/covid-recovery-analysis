# 🦠 COVID-19 Recovery Analysis — What Actually Determines Recovery?

**Author:** Fatima Zahra Boutkhil
**Tools:** Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn)
**Data:** Post COVID Health Effects Dataset

## About
COVID-19 recovery is more complex and counterintuitive than most people assume.
This project explores what truly drives recovery — and the findings challenge 
common assumptions about age, hospitalization, and demographics.

## Questions Explored
1. Does age or gender affect the recovery process?
2. Does exercise speed up COVID recovery?
3. Is COVID severity related to age or gender?
4. Do specific symptoms cluster with certain severity levels?
5. What is the mental health impact post-COVID?
6. Are non-hospitalized patients at risk of ongoing physical problems?

## Key Findings
- Age doesn't slow recovery — but physical activity significantly speeds it up
- Symptoms are better predictors of severity than age or gender
- Hospitalization doesn't guarantee worse long-term outcomes
- Mental health impact post-COVID is significant regardless of severity
- Non-hospitalized patients still face ongoing physical risks worth monitoring

## Main Takeaway
> *"The body's response matters more than demographics.
> Stay active, monitor symptoms, and provide equal follow-up care to all patients."*
